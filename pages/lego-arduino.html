hacking lego motors with arduino

See how you can hack your lego motors and control them via the Arduino IDE program.



I had the need to create a reliable and easy way to connect Lego Motors to Arduino. This project used:
  • Arduino UNO R3
  • NPN (BJT) Transistor
  • Connector Pins
  • Breadboard Wires
  • LEGO Motor
I knew that the 5V that the Arduino outputed wasn't resonable for the motors, so I designed a circuit utilising the Transistor to output 9V when voltage is being passed through the Base. A transistor is like an electronic switch that allows electricity to pass through when a small amount of voltage is passed through the base.

The ouput is connected to the C1 pin of the LEGO motor and the negative (C2) connects to the GND of the 9V battery (see circuit diagram above). To reverse the rotation of the motor, reverse the polarity of the conections (C1 - Negative, C2 - Positive).

In order to connect the Lego motor to the ardiuno, I used a Lego motor extension cord and cut it so the striped cable is able for soldering. I soldered a temporary connector that make it easier to connect the motor connector to a bread board, where the transistor and circuitry is located.
